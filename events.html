Track Cycling Events

The different race formats

There are two broader categories of race formats: sprint races and endurance races. The main sprint events are as follows: sprint, team sprint, keirin and time trial. The main endurance formats are: Individual pursuit, team pursuit, madison, scratch race, points race and omnium.

Sprint

Two to four competitors, but usally two. The start next to each other, but sprint itself doesn't necessarily begin at the starting line. By going slow in the beginning, the riders attempt to trick their opponent into making the first move, allowing them to draft their opponent, to gain an aerodynamic advantage. This advantage lets them save energy, making it easier to overtake in time to cross the finish line first.

Team sprint

Despite its name, the team sprint is not a regular cycling sprint event. Its a three-man time trial for men's events, or a two-woman event over two laps.

Keirin

Meaning "racing cycle" in Japanese, is a a paced event where the riders sprint for victory following a speed controlled start behind a pacer bicycle or motorcycle. The keirin was originally invented in Japan, where it is huge in terms of gambling today.

Track Time Trial

A sprint event in which individual track cyclist compete against the clock to be the fastest over a specified distance.

Individual pursuit

An endurance event in which two track cyclist begin the race with a standing start on oppsite sides of the track. The riders start simultaneously to complete a distance specified distance in the fastest time. The distance is 3 km for women and 4 km for men.

Team pursuit

Almost like the indiviual pursuit, but with two teams of up to four riders compete against eachother. From a standing start they try to ride the specified distance in the fastest time.

Madison

A race in which each team must complete more laps than the other teams in a specified time. Each team's riders alternate over the courseof the race, so that only one rider in each team races at any time. The alternate rider has to be touched by his team mate before taking over.

Scratch race

A race in which all the riders start together and attempt to be the first over the finish line after a specified number of laps. Riders that are lapped and overtaken by the rest of the group are eliminated.

Points race

A 40 km long endurance race, in which a sprint is held every 10 laps. Points are awarded to the top four finishers each lap. The rider with the most points at the end of the race wins.

Omnium

The omnium is a multiple race track cycling event. It has had a variety of formats over the course of history, and the term has recently been adopted in road racing to describe certain multiple-day races.
