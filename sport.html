The Sport of Track Cycling

About the sport of track cycling

Track cycling is a bicycle racing sport. The races are usually held at banked tracks called velodromes.

History

Track cycling is a fairly old sport and existed as early as 1870, if not earlier. Since the start of track cycling, the races have always been held on velodromes, although the tracks weren't standardized like they are today. The most notable historical change in track cycling are the bikes, being far lighter and more aerodynamic today, compared to what they used to be. Same goes with the other equipment that track cyclists use.

Popularity

While track cycling is nowhere near as popular as road cycling, it's not tiny either. It's probably most popular in Europeian countries such as Belgium, France, Germany and the United Kingdom. There track cycling is often used as a means to off season training for road-racers, who also ride the track professionally. The sport would probably be more popular if it was more accesssible, but that would require there to be more velodromes around the world.
